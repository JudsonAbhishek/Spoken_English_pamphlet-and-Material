TENSES (కాలములు)

Tense is a verb form which tells the time of an action or event.

In English, Tenses are mainly three types

  1. 1. Present Tense (వర్తమాన కాలము లేదా ప్రస్తుతానికి సంబంధించినది)
  2. 2. Past Tense (భూతకాలము లేక జరిగిపోయిన దానికి సంబంధించినది)
  3. 3. Future Tense (భవిష్యత్ కాలము లేక జరగబోయే కాలమునకు సంబంధించినది)

Each tense is further divided into four types based on time or situation, making a total of 12 tenses. They are:

PRESENT TENSE :
  1. Present Simple Tense (or) Simple Present Tense (or) Present Indefinite Tense
  2. Present Continuous Tense (or) Present Progressive Tense
  3. Present Perfect Tense
  4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense
PAST TENSE :
  1. Past Simple Tense (or) Simple Past Tense (or) Past Indefinite Tense
  2. Past Continuous Tense (or) Past Progressive Tense
  3. Past Perfect Tense
  4. Past Perfect Continuous Tense
FUTURE TENSE :
  1. Future Simple Tense (or) Simple Future Tense (or) Future Indefinite Tense
  2. Future Continuous Tense (or) Future Progressive Tense
  3. Future Perfect Tense
  4. Future Perfect Continuous Tense

CONJUGATION:

"Different forms of a verb is called conjugation of a verb"

Ex:

(V₁) Go      (V₂) Went      (V₃) Gone
(V₁) Do      (V₂) Did       (V₃) Done
(V₁) Write    (V₂) Wrote     (V₃) Written
V₁ = Present Tense form of a verb (V1, is mainly used for simple Present Tense)
V₂ = Past Tense form of a verb (V2, is mainly used for simple Past Tense)
V₃ = Past Participle form of a verb (V3, is mainly used for all Perfect Tenses and for all passive voices)
V₄ = V₁+ing = Present Participle (V4 is mainly used for on-going actions at Present, Past & Future Tenses)
General Structure :

+

Exp: He eats Fruits
SUBJECT : It is a part of a sentence about whom or which something is said.
Doer of an action (Or) Performer of an action.
(Or) The naming part / heading part of the sentence is called subject.
OBJECT : A Person or thing to which some thing is done.(or) some feeling or thought is directed.
• A Person or thing acted upon by the Subject.
• A Person or thing who receives the result of an action.
VERB : It is a word which expresses the state of doing, being and possessing.
Some Important points or rules to remember
  1. Generally every tense has different helping verbs and main verbs.
  2. We have to add 'not' to the helping verbs to get negative sentences.
  3. We have to put helping verbs before the subjects to get interrogative sentences. (Questions)
